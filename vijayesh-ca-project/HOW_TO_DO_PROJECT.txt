================================================================================
                         STEP 3: EXPLAIN VULNERABILITIES
================================================================================

TELL YOUR AUDIENCE:

"Why are MD5 and SHA-1 CRITICAL risks?"

1. MD5:
   - Can compute 10 BILLION hashes per second on GPU
   - No salt - rainbow tables work instantly
   - Cracked in < 0.001 seconds

2. SHA-1:
   - Similar problems as MD5
   - Deprecated by NIST since 2011
   - Cracked in < 0.01 seconds

3. bcrypt:
   - Has built-in SALT (128-bit random)
   - Work factor (cost=10) = 1024 iterations
   - Would take 3-10+ YEARS to crack
   - SECURE!


================================================================================
                        STEP 4: EXPLAIN THE SALT CONCEPT
================================================================================

TELL YOUR AUDIENCE (simple explanation):

"Think of salt like a random spice added to each person's food. Even if two
people have the same meal (password), the spice (salt) makes them taste
completely different (different hashes)."

SHOW THIS DIAGRAM:

WITHOUT SALT (MD5/SHA-1):
Password "hello" → Hash: a830c6... (always the same!)

WITH SALT (bcrypt):
User1: "hello" + salt1 → Hash1: $2a$10$abc123...
User2: "hello" + salt2 → Hash2: $2a$10$xyz789...

SAME password, DIFFERENT hashes!

WHY THIS MATTERS:
- Without salt: Crack ONE password, get EVERYONE with that password
- With salt: Must crack EACH password individually


================================================================================
                      STEP 5: GIVE RECOMMENDATIONS (NIST-BASED)
================================================================================

TELL YOUR AUDIENCE:

"Based on our audit, here are 3 improvements to the password policy:"

POINT 1: LENGTH OVER COMPLEXITY
────────────────────────────────
❌ OLD: "Must have uppercase, lowercase, number, special character"
✅ NEW: "Minimum 12 characters, no complexity required"

Why? "CorrectHorseBatteryStaple" (25 chars) is stronger than "P@ssw0rd1!"

POINT 2: MODERN HASHING
────────────────────────────────
❌ OLD: MD5, SHA-1, SHA-256 (no salt, fast)
✅ NEW: bcrypt or Argon2id (built-in salt, slow)

Why? bcrypt takes 3-10+ years to crack vs < 1 second for MD5

POINT 3: MFA + BREACH CHECK
────────────────────────────────
❌ OLD: Just password
✅ NEW: Add Multi-Factor Authentication + check HaveIBeenPwned

Why? Even if password cracks, MFA stops attackers

Hash Types:

  $2a$...   → bcrypt  (SECURE - 3-10 years)

The 3 Things That Make Passwords Secure:
  1. SALT (random data added to each password)
  2. WORK FACTOR (how many times to hash)
  3. GOOD ALGORITHM (bcrypt/Argon2, NOT MD5/SHA)

The 3 Things Wrong With MD5/SHA-1:
  1. NO SALT (rainbow tables work)
  2. TOO FAST (billions/second)
  3. NO WORK FACTOR (no delay for attackers)
